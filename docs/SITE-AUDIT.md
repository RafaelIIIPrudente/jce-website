# Site Audit & Rebuild Handoff: JC Electrofields Power System (jcepower.com)

## 1. Summary

JC Electrofields Power System, Inc. ("JCE") is a Philippines-based electrical engineering contractor, consultant, and supplier serving utilities, solar developers, the National Grid Corporation of the Philippines (NGCP), and industrial clients. The site is built on Google Sites using a default template, with content composed largely of embedded raster image cards (text rendered inside JPEGs), Google Slides embeds, and YouTube placeholders. Current overall quality is best described as a functional, late-2000s corporate brochure: information is present but the brand, hierarchy, typography, and trust signaling are all severely below the standard expected of a 28-year-old infrastructure firm with a 124-person team and multi-MW national projects. The biggest opportunity is a complete reframing as a serious editorial engineering portfolio — replacing image-baked text with real typography, using the project list as the primary asset, and building a confident, restrained visual identity around dark green, steel, and white.

## 4. Visual System (Current)

**Color palette** (sampled from screenshots; hex values are best-fit approximations):

- Primary dark green (header bar, section title text, primary button): ~`#1F6F2C`
- Secondary green (logo lockup, accents): ~`#5BA02A`
- Accent orange-red (icon glyphs, location pins, project-card labels): ~`#E2611D`
- Body text dark: ~`#222222`
- Surface white: `#FFFFFF`
- Surface light grey (footer band, alternate sections): ~`#F2F2F2`
- Hero overlay neutral (sky photography blue/grey, used for nearly every page hero): ~`#7DA1B8` photographic neutral
- No defined success/error palette; the form simply uses the host Google Forms red asterisk for required.

**Typography**:

- Header/navigation type: a transitional serif (Google Sites default — visually consistent with "PT Serif" or "Roboto Slab"), used for nav items "Home / About Us / …" at ~17px regular.
- Body text outside of images: a humanist sans (Google Sites "Roboto") at ~16px.
- All section headings, mission/vision blocks, product lists, and service straplines are raster images — typography inside those images is a mix of:
  - A bold geometric sans (used in product/service lists)
  - A casual semi-bold sans for headings ("MISSION & COMMITMENT", "HISTORY", "Mission", "Vision")
  - An outlined display serif for hero page titles like "Solar Farm" and "Distribution Utility" — green stroke, no fill, drop-shadow.
  - A novelty hand-style font with orange fill + white outline + drop-shadow for project category cards ("Electric Distribution Utility, Government and Private Sector")
- No consistent type scale: h-equivalents on different pages range visually from 28px to 48px with no clear rhythm.
- Line-height inside image cards is cramped (~1.2), with justified text causing rivers of whitespace (visible in History block).

**Spacing rhythm**:

- Section vertical padding inconsistent: hero block ~0px gap to first content section on Home; About Us has ~80px between cards; Professional Services has a huge ~120px column of empty space on the right.
- Container max-width approximately 1040–1080px centered.
- Gutter between 2-column grids ~24px.

**Border radius**: Buttons use a tiny radius (~2px). Image cards on the Projects index use a custom torn/beveled-corner clip (decorative, not a radius).

**Shadow usage**: Flat. Project-index cards have a soft drop shadow (~`0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.15)`); everything else is shadow-less.

**Borders**: No visible 1px stroke system. Image cards have implicit borders baked into the JPEG.

**Imagery system**: Full-bleed photography of substations, linesmen, solar farms, and crane assembly. Treatment is raw, unprocessed JPEG — no color grading, no duotone, mixed quality (some are clearly 2010-era 720p; others recent drone aerials). Photo aspect ratios vary widely. The same substation linesmen hero is reused as the page hero on Home, Product & Services, Professional Services, Projects, Solar Farm, Distribution Utility, and Contact Us.

**Iconography**: Two parallel icon vocabularies in conflict:

- Red/orange clip-art-style illustrations (hard-hat figure, factory, gears, target, lightbulb, checklist, location pin) — appear in all rastered cards. Visually consistent with a free icon-pack from ~2015.
- Lucide-style line icons for footer (calendar, envelope, phone, location pin) — thinner, modern.

## 6. Interaction & Motion (Current)

- Sticky top header that remains pinned during scroll.
- Hover state on nav items: subtle color shift; active page has a persistent underline.
- Dropdown menu on "Projects" reveals three child links on hover.
- Image carousels in project tiles auto-advance and respond to dot clicks.
- A small downward chevron sits at the bottom of the About Us hero suggesting "scroll for more".
- No reveal-on-scroll animation, no parallax, no pinned sections, no scroll-triggered counters, no page transitions.
- **Responsive behavior**: Google Sites default — the header collapses to a hamburger on narrow viewports, and 2-column grids stack. Image cards (the bulk of content) cannot reflow because their text is baked into pixels — on mobile this means horizontally scaled JPEGs with unreadable type, a major issue.

## 7. Diagnosis

### Hierarchy problems

- **Every hero is a void.** `/home`, `/product-services`, `/professional-services`, `/projects`, `/contact-us` all use the exact same substation linesmen photo as a full-bleed hero with no headline, no subheadline, and no CTA. The visitor lands with zero confirmation of what page they're on, what JCE does, or what to do next. The Home hero in particular shows only a transparent logo PNG over a photo — there is no value proposition above the fold.
- **No primary CTA exists anywhere on the homepage or subpages.** The only buttons on the entire site are the three "Solar Farm / Distribution Utility / Projects Under NGCP" tiles on `/projects`.
- **All section content has equal weight** because it is delivered as flat JPEGs of the same size and contrast, so the eye has no entry point. On `/home` the YouTube video, the Google Slides embed, and the service strap-lines all compete for the same attention.

### Typography problems

- The site has effectively no typography system because most type is baked into images. This means it cannot reflow, cannot be selected, cannot be translated, cannot be searched, and is invisible to assistive tech.
- At least four conflicting display faces are visible across baked images: a clean bold sans, a casual rounded sans, an outlined serif, and a novelty hand-drawn font. Used together with no system.
- The body text inside the History block is justified with tight line-height and short measure, producing visible rivers and orphans (best seen on `/about-us` History card).
- Where real HTML text does exist (nav, footer), it sits at default Google Sites Roboto 16px with no scale.

### Color problems

- Three competing color systems with no rules: dark-utility-green branding, bright-orange/red illustration icons, plus the green outline-serif hero overlays (e.g., "Solar Farm", "Distribution Utility"). The outline-serif overlays sit on top of a photographic sky and routinely have insufficient contrast — "Projects Under National Grid Corporation of the Philippines (NGCP)" on the NGCP page is nearly unreadable.
- Project-card labels on `/projects` (orange filled, white outlined, drop-shadowed serif/script) are a fourth, dissonant system.
- No neutral grey scale exists for hierarchy; everything is either green, orange-red, or near-black.

### Spacing problems

- `/professional-services` leaves the right ~50% of the canvas empty for the three service strap-lines, producing a page that feels broken on desktop.
- `/home` has hero → list of three projects with almost no top padding (~16px), then huge whitespace between the YouTube row and the slides row.
- No vertical rhythm: section paddings of 16, 48, 80, and 120px all coexist.

### Generic / template patterns to actively move away from

- **The unmodified Google Sites shell.** The header geometry (logo pill, serif nav, magnifying-glass icon, "Google Sites / Report abuse" footer pill on info icon hover) immediately reads as a free-template site, undercutting credibility for an industrial contractor.
- **Embedded Google Slides as content tiles** with the page-counter/kebab/Google Slides badge fully visible — an unmistakable "I built this on a free tool" signal.
- **Embedded raw YouTube players** retaining "Watch on YouTube" overlays and channel branding instead of styled video poster frames.
- **Image-baked text cards with clip-art-style icons** (target, lightbulb, hard-hat figure, factory) — a hallmark of mid-2010s PowerPoint-export web pages.
- **Outlined display-serif page titles** ("Solar Farm", "Distribution Utility") floating in front of photography — a stock free-template flourish.
- **Reused full-bleed hero with no headline.** A common Lovable/template tell.

### Copy problems

- **No homepage value proposition.** The phrase the company wants visitors to remember is hidden in the footer: "We, the JC Electrofields Power System, Inc… We May not be on top, but we're on our way up!… Together with you." This is endearing but is also a self-deprecating tagline ("we may not be on top") on the most important real estate of a B2B contractor site.
- All product/service descriptions are feature lists in shouting all-caps ("SPECIALIST IN AUTOMATION AND CONTROLS.") rather than outcome statements.
- The About Us History paragraph is one ~250-word wall of justified text written in third-person corporate-bio style with grammar issues ("contractor/supplier to all Power Industry sector").
- Sub-headlines repeatedly say "Supply, Delivery, Installation, Testing & Comissioning [sic]" — the same nine words begin almost every project tile across three pages. Typo "Comissioning" appears on the Solar Farm page.
- CTAs that do exist are bare nouns: "Solar Farm", "Electric Distribution Utility", "Projects Under NGCP" — no verbs, no benefit.

### Conversion / UX friction

- No "Request a quote", "Contact us", or "Discuss your project" CTA anywhere outside the dedicated Contact page link in the nav.
- The Contact page requires visitors to be signed into a Google account (the embedded form shows "rflprdnt@gmail.com / Switch account / Not shared"). This is hostile to procurement teams using corporate email tools that aren't Google Workspace.
- No client logos, no project counts, no certifications/ISO badges, no NGCP accreditation marker, no team headcount (it's only mentioned buried in the About paragraph: "124 manpower compliments"). The most credible facts the company has are invisible.
- No filtering, no map, no MW totals on the project pages — visitors must read each card individually.

### Accessibility red flags (visible without devtools)

- Image-baked text is invisible to screen readers; no observed alt text strategy.
- Outlined-serif hero titles over sky photography have insufficient color contrast.
- Tap targets on mobile will fail: the only buttons (Projects tiles) are fine, but everything else is a raster card.
- Embedded Google Form's required-asterisk pattern is the only error signal; no inline validation messages are styled.

## 8. Prescription

### Hierarchy and content reframing

- Replace every image-baked card with real HTML. Every section heading, every product bullet, every "Supply, Delivery, Installation, Testing & Commissioning" line becomes typed text.
- Write a true homepage hero: an editorial H1 such as "Building the Philippines' power grid since 1997." with a 1-line subhead "Substation, transmission and renewable-energy EPC for utilities, NGCP, and industrial plants — 124 engineers across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao." and two CTAs: "See our projects" (primary) and "Start a project" (secondary).
- Drop the existing "We may not be on top, but we're on our way up" line from the footer/copy. Use it nowhere in primary positioning. If retained anywhere, frame it as founder voice on an About page.

### Typography

- Adopt a single editorial pairing: Fraunces (or Instrument Serif) for display headings, paired with Inter (or Geist) for body and UI.
- H1: 64px / 500 weight / -2% letter-spacing / 1.05 line-height. H2: 44px / 500 / -1.5%. H3: 24px / 500. Body: 17px / 400 / 1.6 line-height. Caption/eyebrow: 13px uppercase / 500 / +8% tracking.
- Cap body line-length at 65ch. Switch all justified text to ragged-right.

### Color

- Anchor on a restrained palette: deep forest green primary, warm steel neutrals, restrained accent. Tokens proposed in §9.
- Eliminate the orange-red clip-art icons. Replace with Lucide icons at 1.5px stroke in the primary text color or a single muted accent.

### Imagery

- Stop reusing the same linesmen hero on every page. Each page hero needs its own image — solar farm aerials, substation crane lifts, control-room inter…
