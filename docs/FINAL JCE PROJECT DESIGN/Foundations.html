One visual language. Glass for chrome, solid for content.

A light, frosted-white interface over a soft luminous backdrop with slow-drifting green & orange glow. Framing and navigation are frosted glass; every data-dense surface — tables, ledgers, forms, registers, payslips — sits on high-contrast solid white. Glass is never used where it would compromise reading figures or filling forms.

◇
The defining rule. GLASS FOR CHROME, SOLID FOR CONTENT. Top bars, sidebars, cards, modals and section headers are frosted glass. Tables, ledgers, forms, reports and payslips are always solid. Maximum 3 blurred layers per viewport; everything degrades to an intentional solid fallback. Motion honors prefers-reduced-motion. Text on glass meets WCAG AA.

04 · Status

Status-chip vocabulary

Every document lifecycle in the system renders as a chip. Error states are never color-only — a dot/glyph accompanies the label. Locked is ink-dark with a lock glyph.
